# Race Director Guide

Welcome to RaceLookup's comprehensive platform for race directors and event organizers. This guide covers everything you need to know to successfully manage your events.

## Getting Started

### Create a Business Account

1. [Create a Free Account](https://racelookup.com/register)
2. Set up your business profile
3. Verify your account
4. Start listing your events!

### Business Profile

Your business profile is your organization's hub on RaceLookup:
- Showcase your race timing company or organization
- Display your services and expertise
- Connect with runners and other race directors
- Manage all your events from one dashboard

[Access Dashboard](https://racelookup.com/dashboard)

## Listing Events

### Create Your First Event

1. Navigate to [List Events](https://racelookup.com/web/events)
2. Click "Create New Event"
3. Fill in event details:
   - Event name and description
   - Date and time
   - Location and venue
   - Race distances/categories
   - Registration pricing
4. Configure registration settings
5. Publish your event

## Best Practices

### Before Your Event

1. **Plan Early**: Create your event listing well in advance
2. **Set Competitive Pricing**: Use early bird discounts to drive early registrations
3. **Clear Communication**: Provide detailed event information
4. **Test Registration**: Complete a test registration to ensure everything works
5. **Promote Your Event**: Share on social media and running communities

### During Registration

1. **Monitor Sign-ups**: Track registration numbers regularly
2. **Engage with Participants**: Answer questions promptly
3. **Send Updates**: Keep registrants informed about event details
4. **Manage Capacity**: Set registration limits if needed

### On Race Day

1. **Have Support Ready**: Ensure you have technical support available
2. **Coordinate with Timing**: Confirm timing system integration
3. **Monitor Results**: Ensure results are publishing correctly
4. **Engage with Participants**: Respond to any race day issues quickly

### After the Event

1. **Publish Final Results**: Ensure all results are complete
2. **Share Photos**: Upload and tag race photos
3. **Send Thank Yous**: Thank participants and volunteers
4. **Gather Feedback**: Collect participant feedback for future improvements
5. **Analyze Data**: Review registration and participation metrics
